Early life and education
John Fitzgerald Kennedy was born at 83 Beals Street in Brookline, Massachusetts, on May 29, 1917,[7] to businessman/politician Joseph Patrick "Joe" Kennedy, Sr. (1888�1969) and philanthropist/socialite Rose Elizabeth Fitzgerald (1890�1995). Kennedy defeated Vice President and Republican candidate Richard Nixon in the 1960 U.S. Presidential Election. [3] To date, Kennedy has been the only Roman Catholic president and the only president to have won a Pulitzer Prize.
