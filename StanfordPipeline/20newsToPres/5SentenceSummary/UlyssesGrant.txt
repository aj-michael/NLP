He was, as the symbol of Union victory during the Civil War, their logical candidate for President in 1868. Grant provided neither vigor nor reform. One visitor to the White House noted "a puzzled pathos, as of a man with a problem before him of which he does not understand the terms." At the outbreak of the Civil War, Grant was working in his father's leather store in Galena, Illinois. When he was elected, the American people hoped for an end to turmoil.
