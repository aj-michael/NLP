Suddenly these and a host of other wartime problems became Truman's to solve when, on April 12, 1945, he became President. Truman was born in Lamar, Missouri, in 1884. He told reporters, "I felt like the moon, the stars, and all the planets had fallen on me." Active in the Democratic Party, Truman was elected a judge of the Jackson County Court (an administrative position) in 1922.
